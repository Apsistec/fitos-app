## RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a healthcare provider or other entity subject to the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA") and the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act ("HITECH Act");

WHEREAS, Business Associate provides fitness coaching and wellness technology services to Covered Entity;

WHEREAS, in connection with such services, Business Associate may create, receive, maintain, or transmit Protected Health Information ("PHI") on behalf of Covered Entity;

WHEREAS, Covered Entity and Business Associate intend to protect the privacy and security of PHI in accordance with HIPAA, the HITECH Act, and their implementing regulations (collectively, the "HIPAA Rules");

NOW THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual covenants and agreements herein contained, the parties agree as follows:

## ARTICLE 1: DEFINITIONS

1.1 **"Protected Health Information" or "PHI"** means information that:
   - Relates to the past, present, or future physical or mental health or condition of an individual;
   - Relates to the provision of health care to an individual; or
   - Relates to the past, present, or future payment for the provision of health care to an individual;
   - And that identifies the individual or for which there is a reasonable basis to believe can be used to identify the individual.

1.2 **"Electronic Protected Health Information" or "ePHI"** means PHI that is transmitted by or maintained in electronic media.

1.3 **"Security Incident"** means the attempted or successful unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of information or interference with system operations in an information system.

1.4 **"Breach"** means the acquisition, access, use, or disclosure of PHI in a manner not permitted under the HIPAA Privacy Rule that compromises the security or privacy of the PHI.

1.5 **"Unsecured PHI"** means PHI that is not secured through the use of technology or methodology specified by the Secretary of HHS in guidance.

1.6 **"Required by Law"** shall have the meaning given in 45 CFR § 164.103.

1.7 **"Secretary"** means the Secretary of the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services ("HHS").

1.8 Other capitalized terms used but not defined herein shall have the meanings set forth in the HIPAA Rules.

## ARTICLE 2: PERMITTED USES AND DISCLOSURES OF PHI

2.1 **Permitted Uses and Disclosures**

Business Associate may use and disclose PHI only to the extent necessary to perform the Services specified in the underlying Service Agreement between the parties, and as otherwise permitted by this Agreement and the HIPAA Rules.

2.2 **Specific Permitted Uses**

Business Associate may use and disclose PHI for the following purposes:

a) **Fitness Coaching Services:** To provide personalized fitness coaching, workout programming, nutrition guidance, and progress tracking to individuals referred by Covered Entity;

b) **Health Data Analysis:** To analyze workout adherence, nutrition compliance, and fitness outcomes to improve service delivery;

c) **Communication:** To communicate with individuals about their fitness programs, appointments, and progress;

d) **Administrative Activities:** To carry out Business Associate's legal responsibilities or perform data aggregation services as permitted under 45 CFR § 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B);

e) **De-identified Data:** To create, use, and disclose de-identified data in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.514(a)-(c);

f) **Limited Data Sets:** To create, use, and disclose limited data sets in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.514(e).

2.3 **Business Associate's Management and Administration**

Business Associate may use PHI for the proper management and administration of Business Associate or to carry out the legal responsibilities of Business Associate, provided that:
- Such use is required by law; or
- Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the person to whom the information is disclosed that it will be held confidentially and used or further disclosed only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed, and the person notifies Business Associate of any instances of which it becomes aware that the confidentiality of the information has been breached.

2.4 **Data Aggregation Services**

Business Associate may use PHI to provide data aggregation services relating to the health care operations of Covered Entity as permitted by 45 CFR § 164.504(e)(2)(i)(B).

## ARTICLE 3: OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

3.1 **Prohibited Uses and Disclosures**

Business Associate shall not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted or required by this Agreement or as Required by Law.

3.2 **Appropriate Safeguards**

Business Associate shall implement and maintain appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to prevent use or disclosure of PHI other than as provided for by this Agreement, in compliance with the Security Rule at 45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C.

3.3 **Reporting of Improper Uses and Disclosures**

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any use or disclosure of PHI not provided for by this Agreement, including any Security Incident or Breach of Unsecured PHI, of which Business Associate becomes aware.

Such report shall be made:
- Without unreasonable delay
- In no case later than **10 business days** after discovery
- In writing to: [Covered Entity Privacy Officer Contact Information]

The report shall include, to the extent known:
a) Identification of each individual whose PHI has been or is reasonably believed to have been accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed;
b) A brief description of what happened, including the date of the incident and the date of discovery;
c) A description of the types of PHI involved;
d) A description of the investigation and mitigation efforts undertaken;
e) A description of corrective actions being taken to prevent future occurrences;
f) Any other information Covered Entity may reasonably request.

3.4 **Business Associate's Agents**

Business Associate shall ensure that any agent, including a subcontractor, to whom it provides PHI received from, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, agrees to the same restrictions and conditions that apply to Business Associate with respect to such PHI through a written agreement that meets the requirements of 45 CFR § 164.504(e).

3.5 **Access to PHI**

Business Associate shall provide access to PHI in a Designated Record Set to Covered Entity or, as directed by Covered Entity, to an Individual, in the time and manner designated by Covered Entity to meet the requirements of 45 CFR § 164.524.

If Business Associate maintains PHI in electronic format, Business Associate shall provide such access in electronic format as required by 45 CFR § 164.524(c)(2).

3.6 **Amendment of PHI**

Business Associate shall make any amendments to PHI in a Designated Record Set that Covered Entity directs or agrees to pursuant to 45 CFR § 164.526, at the request of Covered Entity or an Individual, within **10 business days** of receipt of such request.

3.7 **Accounting of Disclosures**

Business Associate shall document all disclosures of PHI and information related to such disclosures as would be required for Covered Entity to respond to a request by an Individual for an accounting of disclosures of PHI in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.528.

Business Associate shall provide to Covered Entity or an Individual, within **10 business days** of a request, information collected in accordance with this Section to permit Covered Entity to respond to an accounting request.

3.8 **Availability of Books and Records**

Business Associate shall make its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI available to the Secretary for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with the HIPAA Rules, within **10 business days** of request.

3.9 **Minimum Necessary**

Business Associate shall, to the extent practical, limit its use, disclosure, or request of PHI to the minimum necessary to accomplish the intended purpose of the use, disclosure, or request, in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.502(b) and § 164.514(d).

3.10 **Data Integrity and Availability**

Business Associate shall implement policies and procedures to ensure the integrity and availability of ePHI, and to protect against reasonably anticipated threats or hazards to the security or integrity of such information.

## ARTICLE 4: OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

4.1 **Permitted Uses and Disclosures**

Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under the HIPAA Rules if done by Covered Entity.

4.2 **Notice of Privacy Practices**

Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any limitation(s) in its Notice of Privacy Practices that may affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

4.3 **Permission to Use or Disclose**

Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any changes in, or revocation of, permission by an Individual to use or disclose PHI, to the extent that such changes may affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

4.4 **Restrictions on Use or Disclosure**

Covered Entity shall notify Business Associate of any restriction to the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.522, to the extent that such restriction may affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

## ARTICLE 5: TERM AND TERMINATION

5.1 **Term**

This Agreement shall become effective on the Effective Date and shall terminate when all PHI provided by Covered Entity to Business Associate, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, is destroyed or returned to Covered Entity, or, if it is infeasible to return or destroy such PHI, protections are extended to such information in accordance with Section 5.3(c).

5.2 **Termination for Cause**

Upon Covered Entity's knowledge of a material breach by Business Associate, Covered Entity shall:

a) Provide an opportunity for Business Associate to cure the breach or end the violation and terminate this Agreement if Business Associate does not cure the breach or end the violation within **30 days**; or

b) Immediately terminate this Agreement if Business Associate has breached a material term of this Agreement and cure is not possible.

5.3 **Effect of Termination**

Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Business Associate shall:

a) **Return or Destroy PHI:** Return to Covered Entity or, if agreed to by Covered Entity, destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity, or created or received by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity, that Business Associate still maintains in any form. Business Associate shall retain no copies of the PHI.

b) **Subcontractors:** Ensure that all subcontractors and agents return or destroy all PHI in accordance with subsection (a).

c) **Infeasibility:** If return or destruction of PHI is infeasible, Business Associate shall:
   - Notify Covered Entity in writing of the conditions that make return or destruction infeasible;
   - Extend the protections of this Agreement to such PHI and limit further uses and disclosures to those purposes that make the return or destruction infeasible, for so long as Business Associate maintains such PHI; and
   - Not use or disclose such PHI for any purpose other than as permitted by the HIPAA Rules.

5.4 **Survival**

The obligations of Business Associate under Sections 3.2, 3.9, 3.10, and 5.3 shall survive the termination of this Agreement.

## ARTICLE 6: BREACH NOTIFICATION

6.1 **Discovery and Notification**

Business Associate shall, following the discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI, notify Covered Entity of such Breach without unreasonable delay and in no case later than **10 business days** after discovery.

6.2 **Content of Notification**

The notification shall include, to the extent possible:
a) Identification of each individual whose Unsecured PHI has been, or is reasonably believed to have been, accessed, acquired, used, or disclosed during the Breach;
b) A brief description of what happened, including the date of the Breach and the date of discovery;
c) A description of the types of Unsecured PHI involved (such as full name, Social Security number, date of birth, home address, account number, etc.);
d) A brief description of what Business Associate is doing to investigate the Breach, to mitigate losses, and to protect against further Breaches;
e) Any steps individuals should take to protect themselves from potential harm resulting from the Breach;
f) Contact procedures for individuals to ask questions or learn additional information.

6.3 **Cooperation**

Business Associate shall cooperate with Covered Entity in meeting Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR § 164.404 et seq., including providing Covered Entity with all information reasonably requested by Covered Entity to fulfill its breach notification obligations.

6.4 **Breach Investigation and Mitigation**

Business Associate shall conduct a timely and thorough investigation of the Breach and shall take appropriate steps to mitigate, to the extent practicable, any harmful effects of the Breach.

## ARTICLE 7: INDEMNIFICATION

7.1 **Indemnification by Business Associate**

Business Associate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Covered Entity, its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any and all losses, liabilities, damages, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees) arising out of or resulting from:

a) Any breach by Business Associate of its obligations under this Agreement;
b) Any breach of the HIPAA Rules caused by Business Associate;
c) Any negligent or wrongful act or omission of Business Associate, its employees, agents, or subcontractors in connection with the performance of services under the Service Agreement;
d) Any Breach of Unsecured PHI caused by Business Associate.

7.2 **Limitation**

The indemnification obligations set forth in this Article shall not apply to the extent that losses, liabilities, damages, costs, or expenses are caused by the sole negligence or willful misconduct of Covered Entity.

## ARTICLE 8: INSURANCE

8.1 **Required Coverage**

Business Associate shall obtain and maintain during the term of this Agreement the following insurance coverage:

a) **Cyber Liability Insurance:** Minimum coverage of $2,000,000 per occurrence and $5,000,000 aggregate, covering:
   - Network security liability
   - Privacy liability
   - Breach response costs
   - Regulatory defense and penalties
   - Media liability

b) **Professional Liability Insurance (Errors & Omissions):** Minimum coverage of $2,000,000 per occurrence and $5,000,000 aggregate

c) **General Liability Insurance:** Minimum coverage of $1,000,000 per occurrence and $2,000,000 aggregate

8.2 **Proof of Insurance**

Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with certificates of insurance evidencing such coverage within 10 days of the Effective Date and annually thereafter. Covered Entity shall be named as an additional insured on all policies except professional liability.

8.3 **Notice of Cancellation**

Business Associate shall provide Covered Entity with at least 30 days' prior written notice of any cancellation, non-renewal, or material modification of the required insurance coverage.

## ARTICLE 9: AUDIT AND MONITORING

9.1 **Right to Audit**

Covered Entity or its authorized representatives shall have the right, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours, to inspect Business Associate's facilities, systems, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI to monitor compliance with this Agreement.

9.2 **Frequency**

Such audits may be conducted no more than once per year unless:
- Covered Entity has reasonable belief that Business Associate has breached this Agreement;
- Required by the Secretary; or
- Required by applicable law.

9.3 **Cooperation**

Business Associate shall fully cooperate with any such inspection or audit and shall make available all requested information, books, records, and facilities.

9.4 **Costs**

Each party shall bear its own costs of any audit, except that Business Associate shall bear all costs of audits conducted as a result of a breach or suspected breach by Business Associate.

## APPENDIX A: TECHNICAL SAFEGUARDS

Business Associate implements the following technical safeguards in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.312:

### A.1 Access Control (§ 164.312(a)(1))

**Unique User Identification (Required):**
- Every user has a unique user ID
- No shared accounts
- Email-based authentication

**Emergency Access Procedure (Required):**
- Break-glass admin account
- Requires two-person authorization
- All emergency actions logged and reviewed

**Automatic Logoff (Addressable):**
- 15-minute idle timeout (HIPAA standard)
- 2-minute warning before logout
- Session cleared on logout

**Encryption and Decryption (Addressable):**
- AES-256 encryption at rest
- TLS 1.3 encryption in transit
- End-to-end encryption for messaging

### A.2 Audit Controls (§ 164.312(b))

**Implementation:**
- Comprehensive audit logging system
- All PHI access logged automatically
- Immutable audit trail (no edits/deletes)
- 7-year retention policy

**What We Log:**
- User accessing PHI
- What PHI was accessed
- When access occurred
- Action performed (read, create, update, delete)
- Access reason (treatment, payment, operations, research)
- Device and location information

**Monitoring:**
- Real-time suspicious activity detection
- After-hours access alerts
- Excessive access pattern detection
- Daily automated compliance reports

### A.3 Integrity Controls (§ 164.312(c)(1))

**Mechanism to Authenticate ePHI (Addressable):**
- Database constraints prevent invalid data
- Input validation on all forms
- Data corruption detection
- Regular integrity checks
- Cryptographic checksums
- Digital signatures for critical data

### A.4 Person or Entity Authentication (§ 164.312(d))

**Implementation:**
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA) for all staff
- MFA required for sensitive PHI access
- Password requirements:
  - Minimum 12 characters
  - Must include: uppercase, lowercase, number, special character
  - Cannot contain username or email
  - 90-day expiration for admin accounts
- Biometric authentication available (mobile app)
- SSO support for enterprise clients (SAML/OIDC)

### A.5 Transmission Security (§ 164.312(e)(1))

**Integrity Controls (Addressable):**
- HTTPS prevents tampering
- Request/response validation
- Error handling without PHI leakage

**Encryption (Addressable):**
- TLS 1.3 for all API calls
- HTTPS enforced (HSTS headers)
- No unencrypted PHI transmission

## APPENDIX B: ADMINISTRATIVE SAFEGUARDS

Business Associate implements the following administrative safeguards in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.308:

### B.1 Security Management Process (§ 164.308(a)(1))

**Risk Analysis (Required):**
- Annual security risk assessment conducted
- Documented in security risk assessment report
- Identifies threats to PHI confidentiality, integrity, availability

**Risk Management (Required):**
- Security policies and procedures implemented
- Regular security updates and patches
- Incident response plan in place

**Sanction Policy (Required):**
- Disciplinary action for HIPAA violations
- Documented in employee handbook
- Ranges from warning to termination

**Information System Activity Review (Required):**
- Daily automated monitoring of audit logs
- Weekly review of suspicious activity alerts
- Monthly compliance reports generated

### B.2 Assigned Security Responsibility (§ 164.308(a)(2))

**Security Officer (Required):**
- Designated Security Officer appointed
- Responsible for HIPAA compliance
- Oversees security measures
- Conducts security training
- Manages security incidents

### B.3 Workforce Security (§ 164.308(a)(3))

**Authorization and/or Supervision (Addressable):**
- Role-based access control (RBAC)
- Minimum necessary access principle
- Regular access reviews (quarterly)

**Workforce Clearance Procedure (Addressable):**
- Background checks for all employees
- Signed confidentiality agreements
- HIPAA training before PHI access

**Termination Procedures (Addressable):**
- Access revoked within 1 hour of termination
- Return of all devices and credentials
- Exit interview covering HIPAA obligations

### B.4 Information Access Management (§ 164.308(a)(4))

**Access Authorization (Addressable):**
- Formal authorization process
- Written approval from Security Officer
- Documented in access control matrix

**Access Establishment and Modification (Addressable):**
- Unique user IDs for each user
- No shared accounts
- MFA required for PHI access
- Change request process with approval workflow
- Audit trail of all access changes

### B.5 Security Awareness and Training (§ 164.308(a)(5))

**Security Reminders (Addressable):**
- Quarterly security awareness emails
- In-app security tips
- Security awareness posters

**Protection from Malicious Software (Addressable):**
- Antivirus on all workstations
- Regular security updates
- Web application firewall (WAF)

**Log-in Monitoring (Addressable):**
- Failed login attempt tracking
- Account lockout after 5 failures
- Suspicious login alerts

**Password Management (Addressable):**
- Minimum 12 characters
- Complexity requirements enforced
- 90-day expiration for admin accounts
- Password history (last 5 passwords)

### B.6 Security Incident Procedures (§ 164.308(a)(6))

**Response and Reporting (Required):**
- Incident response plan documented
- 24-hour internal reporting requirement
- Breach assessment within 48 hours
- Notification procedures in place

### B.7 Contingency Plan (§ 164.308(a)(7))

**Data Backup Plan (Required):**
- Automated daily backups (Supabase)
- Encrypted backup storage
- Quarterly backup restoration testing

**Disaster Recovery Plan (Required):**
- Recovery Time Objective (RTO): 4 hours
- Recovery Point Objective (RPO): 24 hours
- Documented DR procedures

**Emergency Mode Operation Plan (Required):**
- Emergency access procedures
- Break-glass account with audit logging
- Post-emergency review required

**Testing and Revision Procedure (Addressable):**
- Annual disaster recovery drill
- Plan updated after each test
- Lessons learned documented

### B.8 Evaluation (§ 164.308(a)(8))

**Periodic Technical and Nontechnical Evaluation (Addressable):**
- Annual security evaluation
- Quarterly access reviews
- Monthly compliance audits
- Penetration testing (annual)

### B.9 Business Associate Contracts and Other Arrangements (§ 164.308(b)(1))

**Written Contract or Other Arrangement (Required):**
Business Associate maintains written agreements with all subcontractors and vendors who may have access to PHI, including:
- Supabase (database hosting)
- Stripe (payment processing)
- AWS/Cloud providers (infrastructure)
- Any vendor accessing PHI
